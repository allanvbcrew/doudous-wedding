Travel


How to get to Islay : Islay can be reached by ferry from Kennacraig on the mainland or by flying into Islay Airport from Glasgow. We recommend booking your travel in advance to ensure availability.

By plane

To get to Islay from Glasgow by plane, take a direct flight from Glasgow Airport to Islay Airport with Loganair. The flight duration is approximately 45 minutes, and flights operate two times a day, typically in the morning, and late afternoon. Be sure to check the Loganair website for the most up-to-date schedule and to book your tickets. : www.loganair.co.uk

By ferry

To reach Islay from Glasgow by ferry, drive or take a bus to Kennacraig, a 2.5-hour journey. From Kennacraig, CalMac ferries run to Port Ellen or Port Askaig, taking about 2 hours. The ferry ride offers scenic views of the Scottish coast. Check the CalMac website for current schedules and to book your trip : www.calmac.co.uk

International travel

Non-UK citizens traveling to the UK should check visa requirements based on their nationality. EU citizens can visit the UK for up to 6 months without a visa for tourism, family visits, or short business trips. French citizens must use a passport for entry, as ID cards are no longer accepted. Ensure your passport is valid for the duration of your stay. For the latest information and entry requirements, Visit the UK Home Office website.

Travel tips

Car Rental Information

Glasgow

  • Locations: Available at Glasgow Airport and in the city center.
  • Booking: Reserve in advance online to get better rates and availability. Consider renting a car for flexibility if traveling around Scotland before heading to Islay.

Edinburgh

  • Locations: Available at Edinburgh Airport and throughout the city.
  • Considerations: Parking and traffic can be tricky in Edinburgh. Use public transport for city exploration and pick up a rental car when leaving the city.

Islay

  • Rental Options: Limited rental agencies; book early. Local options like Islay Car Hire or larger companies at Islay Airport are available.
  • Tip: Renting a car is essential on Islay for accessing distilleries and remote spots.
