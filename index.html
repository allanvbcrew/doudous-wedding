Travel


Well, you got it, you'll have to join us on our amazing island for the party. For those of you joining us from Europe, you'll need to first, make it to Scotland!
Then you can travel to Islay :) Islay can be reached by ferry from Kennacraig on the mainland or by flying into Islay Airport from Glasgow. We recommend booking your travel in advance to ensure availability.
Wherever you come from, we've tried to provide some tips below to make you trip as easy and clear as possible.


By plane From Glasgow Airport (GLA)

  • Direct Flights: There are daily flights from Glasgow Airport to Islay Airport (ILY). The flight takes around 45 minutes and offers beautiful aerial views of Scotland’s west coast and the Hebrides. Upon arrival, taxis and car rentals are available to transport you to your accommodation or the wedding venue.
  • Airline: Loganair operates this route, and it’s recommended to book tickets early, especially in the summer months when Islay is a popular destination. : www.loganair.co.uk

By Car and Ferry from Kennacraig Ferry Terminal

  • From Glasgow: Drive west on the M8. Then, take the A82 north towards Loch Lomond. Continue along the A83, passing through Inveraray and Lochgilphead, until you reach the Kennacraig Ferry Terminal. This scenic drive takes approximately 2.5 hours.
  • Ferry from Kennacraig to Islay: Ferries are operated by Caledonian MacBrayne (CalMac) and sail to either Port Askaig or Port Ellen on Islay. The ferry journey takes around 2 hours.
  • Booking: It’s important to book your ferry in advance. You can check the ferry schedule and book tickets on the CalMac website. www.calmac.co.uk

Accommodation



Portnahaven (Wedding Venue Location)
For ultimate convenience, staying right in Portnahaven is the best option, as this is where the wedding will take place. A peaceful coastal village, Portnahaven offers a few quaint self-catering cottages and guesthouses within walking distance of the wedding venue. This is perfect if you want to avoid driving altogether and enjoy the charming seaside atmosphere of the village both before and after the wedding. Accommodation options in Portnahaven may be limited, so we recommend booking early to secure a spot right at the heart of the celebration.

Port Charlotte (15 minutes from Portnahaven)
Just a 15-minute drive from Portnahaven, Port Charlotte offers a selection of charming accommodations, including cozy B&Bs, guesthouses, and small hotels. One popular option is the Port Charlotte Hotel, which provides beautiful seaside views and a warm, welcoming atmosphere. This village is perfect if you're looking for a quiet, scenic spot with easy access to the wedding.

Bruichladdich (20 minutes from Portnahaven)
A short20-minute drive along the coast brings you to Bruichladdich oa quiet village famous for its distillery. Here, you’ll find a handful of self-catering cottages and guesthouses perfect for families or groups who prefer the freedom to cater for themselves. With its peaceful setting and stunning views over Loch Indaal, Bruichladdich offers a relaxing retreat after the wedding festivities.

Bowmore (30 minutes from Portnahaven)
For those looking for more amenities, Bowmore, the island’s main town, is just 30 minutes away. Bowmore offers the widest range of accommodations, including hotels, self-catering options, and B&Bs. Popular places to stay include the Bowmore Hotel and the Harbour Inn, both conveniently located near shops, restaurants, and the famous Bowmore Distillery. Staying in Bowmore is perfect if you’re planning to explore more of Islay during your visit.
